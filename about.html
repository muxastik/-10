Plants are the eukaryotes that comprise the kingdom Plantae; they are predominantly photosynthetic.
This means that they obtain their energy from sunlight,
using chloroplasts derived from endosymbiosis with cyanobacteria to produce sugars from carbon dioxide and water,
using the green pigment chlorophyll. Exceptions are parasitic plants that have lost
the genes for chlorophyll and photosynthesis, and obtain their energy from other
plants or fungi. Most plants are multicellular, except for some green algae.
